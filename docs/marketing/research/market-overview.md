# Market Overview — Clinic-Management Software in Israel (2026)

**Purpose:** Source research for the comparison / listicle content (Layer C of the SEO+GEO strategy — "התוכנות הטובות לניהול קליניקה בישראל (2026)"). This is a fair, honest survey — every product below has real strengths and is a legitimate choice for some clinics. It is *not* a hit piece. Claims are sourced; anything not verifiable from a public page is flagged.

**Access date for all sources:** 2026-07-12
**Currency notes:** Israeli prices are ₪ (ILS) and, where the vendor states it, quoted *before* VAT. Israeli VAT is 18% (since 1 Jan 2025). International prices are the vendor's own USD; approximate ₪ conversions use ≈3.7 ILS/USD and are indicative only, not authoritative.

---

## The one pattern that defines the market

**Every product surveyed — Israeli and international — is cloud + subscription.** Not one offers an offline / on-device / pay-once model where the clinic's data physically stays on the practitioner's own machine. The international products additionally lack Hebrew, RTL layout, and Israeli tax-invoice (חשבונית מס) compliance. This is the structural gap ClinicFlow's positioning targets (offline, pay-once, data-stays-local, תיקון 13). Use this section as the framing of the listicle — but keep individual competitor sections honest and generous, because they win on breadth, polish, integrations, and ecosystem.

## Israeli products

### Tipulog (טיפולוג) — the cloud market leader
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud, subscription. Positions itself as "תוכנת הענן המובילה בישראל" (leading Israeli cloud clinic software).
- **Pricing (stated, ex-VAT):** ₪75/mo — "Getting Started", 1 user, up to 20 active patients, 1GB. ₪99/mo — unlimited patients, 2GB. ₪299/mo — clinics/treatment centers, 2 users, 5GB. ₪395/mo — clinics & physicians, 2 users, 5GB. Extra users ₪45 (clinics) / ₪35 (physicians). SMS/WhatsApp, digital invoicing, prescriptions, questionnaires, letters and payment processing are **"בתמחור נפרד" (priced separately)** on lower tiers. 30-day free trial, no credit card.
- **Target professions:** Physicians, clinics, and the full range of licensed therapy professions.
- **Standout strengths:** Strongest brand recognition in the Israeli therapist market; billing to funders (health funds/קופות, Ministry of Defense/משהב"ט, insurers); digital invoices; continuous backup + encryption; Google Calendar sync; broad professional coverage. **Acquired by Arbox in 2025**, adding a path to Arbox's wider business-management/growth platform.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Cloud-only, subscription. Entry tier caps at 20 patients. Real monthly cost climbs once invoicing/SMS/prescriptions (billed separately) are added, so the ₪75/₪99 headline understates a working setup. +VAT on top.
- **Sources:** https://tipulog.co.il/ · https://tipulog.co.il/מחירון · Arbox acquisition referenced on vendor/press coverage (2025).

### My-Cliniq — flat, simple, unlimited patients
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud SaaS, subscription. Israeli.
- **Pricing:** Flat **₪79/mo +VAT** across all four plans, differing by scaling axis: *Private* (single user), *Based/מבוסס* (per user, multi-user), *Spaced/מרווח* (per room), *Institute Plus/מכון פלוס* (per user, full center). All tiers include unlimited active patients. First month free, no credit card.
- **Target professions:** Private therapy clinics and treatment centers — therapists, doctors, practitioners.
- **Standout strengths:** Very simple, predictable pricing; unlimited patients on every tier; fast documentation UX (vendor claims 2–3 min per session); one integrated system (calendar + EMR + invoices + funders + reports); strong privacy/confidentiality messaging; useful per-room billing option for single-operator multi-room clinics.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Cloud-only, subscription. +VAT. Cost scales per user/room for larger teams. Less of a marketing/growth toolset than Medform.
- **Sources:** https://www.my-cliniq.com/ · https://www.my-cliniq.com/pricing/ · https://www.my-cliniq.com/faqs/

### Medform — clinic software with growth/marketing built in
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud only, subscription.
- **Pricing:** Public site routes to a pricelist page (https://medform.co.il/pricelist/) that did not surface a figure on access. Secondary Israeli review lists a **single plan at ₪390/mo (2 users, 8GB, invoicing/reports, CRM, online booking, calendar sync, feedback, website promotion)** with credit-card processing as a paid add-on — treat as indicative and verify. A **free program / "Alpha"** grants full access (records, billing, patient comms) in exchange for feedback.
- **Target professions:** Dental, aesthetics, private clinics; physicians, dermatologists, therapists, psychologists, nurses, clinic managers.
- **Standout strengths:** Growth-oriented — one-click clinic **image website**, integrated CRM and lead-to-patient tracking, built-in advertising/SEO/automated campaigns, an **AI assistant** for WhatsApp/SMS communication and scheduling, plus an accounting/invoicing module. Genuinely differentiated for clinics that want marketing + practice management in one place.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Higher entry price than the ₪50–₪99 pack (if ₪390 holds); cloud-only, subscription; the marketing surface area can be overkill for a solo therapist who just wants records + calendar + invoices.
- **Sources:** https://medform.co.il/ · https://medform.co.il/about/ · https://medform.co.il/alpha/ · pricing figure via https://www.greeninvoice.co.il/magazine/ (Israeli review) — verify.

### Jade — mobile-first for solo practitioners
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud with strong native mobile apps (iOS + Android). Israeli (Tel Aviv; founder Asaf Feldman).
- **Pricing:** **Not publicly listed** — the pricing page (jade.co.il/pricing) blocked automated access (HTTP 403) and no figure surfaced in secondary sources. Some listings imply a free tier; unconfirmed. Verify directly before publishing any number.
- **Target professions:** Independent/solo therapists, doctors, and small clinics.
- **Standout strengths:** Genuinely mobile-first — run the practice from a phone or tablet, not just a desktop. Smart treatment calendar with Google sync; patient files with history, notes, templates, and tags for fast search; invoices/receipts with payment tracking and treatment-series management; encryption for records. Clean and approachable for one-person practices.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Cloud-only, subscription. Pricing opacity (no public figures). Oriented to solo/small — lighter fit for multi-clinic or enterprise needs.
- **Sources:** https://www.jade.co.il/ · https://jade.co.il/welcome · https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=il.co.jade.app · https://apps.apple.com/il/app/private-practice-manager/id1453084596

### CliniCloud (קליניקלאוד) — cheapest entry, boutique support
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud, subscription. Positions as a "boutique" system built with practicing therapists.
- **Pricing (ex-VAT, monthly / annual):** Basic ₪50 / ₪600 · Plus ₪70 / ₪840 · Advance ₪95 / ₪1,140 · Doctors ₪150 / ₪1,800 · Business ₪195+ (2 users; extra users ₪42 each). 5% annual discount. **Unlimited patients on every tier**, including the ₪50 Basic. One free month, no credit card.
- **Target professions:** Therapists, doctors, clinics, treatment centers, and associations/NPOs.
- **Standout strengths:** Lowest published entry price in the market (₪50) *with* unlimited patients; WhatsApp/SMS messaging built in; digital treatment summaries, session templates, payment tracking, calendar + reminders, appointment confirmations, task management, Google Calendar sync on all tiers; boutique = personal, responsive support.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Cloud-only, subscription. CRM, questionnaires/feedback, and inventory are gated to higher tiers. Boutique vendor = smaller scale/roadmap than the larger players.
- **Sources:** https://www.clinicloud.co.il/ · https://www.clinicloud.co.il/מחירים · https://www.clinicloud.co.il/אבטחת-מידע

### DOCTOR Clinix (דוקטור) — the established enterprise incumbent
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud ("advanced cloud system," built to Israeli cybersecurity standards), subscription/enterprise licensing. Company: Doctor Medical Systems Ltd., **founded 1984 (35+ years)**; states **100,000+ users**; references hospital clients including Ichilov and Assaf Harofeh.
- **Pricing:** **Not disclosed** — enterprise/quote-based sales.
- **Target professions:** The broadest span — independent therapists, dental, plastic surgery, physiotherapy, psychology/psychiatry, medical/nursing schools, multi-specialty centers, and hospitals.
- **Standout strengths:** Most established and enterprise-grade option; deepest feature set — EHR + CRM + ERP + lead system; in-software EMV secure card payments; treatment plans/series and price quotes; diagnoses, medications, and prescriptions with **drug-interaction warnings**; digitally signed bilingual (Hebrew/English) documents; foreign-currency support; daily cash register, revenue dashboards, advanced reporting. Hospital-grade references lend credibility for large organizations.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Opaque, quote-based pricing (likely the most expensive and the heaviest to deploy); complexity and enterprise orientation make it overkill for a solo practitioner; cloud + subscription/enterprise licensing.
- **Sources:** https://doctor-clinix.com/ · https://doctor-clinix.com/about/ · https://doctor-clinix.com/products-2/ · https://doctor-clinix.com/faq/

### Plexidoc (פלקסידוק) — physician- and surgery-oriented
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud, subscription. Built by Tradis Gat Ltd., "developed by doctors for doctors" (Petah Tikva). **ISO 27799** (health-information security) compliant.
- **Pricing:** **Quote-based.** Five tiers — Plexi lite, Plexi Expert, GOLD, GOLD+, Plexi-Customize — differentiated by user count and storage (1–10GB) with SMS allocations of 200–1,500/mo. Contact for a quote.
- **Target professions:** Physicians and medical practices, with notable surgical support.
- **Standout strengths:** Physician depth — electronic records organized by medical condition; a **surgery module** with pre/post-operative tracking; automated SMS reminders and confirmations; WhatsApp visit summaries; a **patient portal** with self-scheduling and access to visit summaries; clinic-website online booking; analytics/reporting; ISO 27799 security posture.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Pricing opacity (quote only). Some capabilities (legal documents, advanced AI scheduling, certain digital integrations) cost extra on top of the base tier. Cloud + subscription.
- **Sources:** https://plexidoc.com/ · https://plexidoc.com/trail/ · https://www.facebook.com/Plexidoc/

### ClinicaOnline (קליניקה אונליין) — human + veterinary, operations-heavy
- **Model / deploy:** Cloud, subscription. One of the few Israeli systems that explicitly covers **veterinary** clinics alongside human medical clinics (vet client portal with pet vaccination records and lab results).
- **Pricing:** **Not published** on site — contact for a quote; onboarding requires a clinic code, so registration is gated at the clinic level.
- **Target professions:** Physicians and veterinarians; multi-clinic networks.
- **Standout strengths:** Deep operational feature set — inventory management and purchasing, laboratory-test integration, prescription generation, financial tracking with credit-card processing, accounting linkage, staff management, and multi-clinic network management; email/SMS reminders; integrates with InforUMobile for multichannel patient messaging. Strong fit where inventory/labs matter.
- **Honest weaknesses:** Pricing opacity and gated onboarding. Its veterinary coverage is lighter than Israel's **dedicated** vet systems (VetKey, Rapid Vet) — good for a small vet clinic with basic needs, less so for a full veterinary practice. Cloud + subscription.
- **Sources:** https://ww2.clinicaonline.co.il/ · https://www.inforu.co.il/אינטגרציה-בין-inforumobile-ל-clinicaonline/ · (vet-niche context: https://www.vetkey.co.il/ · https://www.rapid-image.com/rapid-vet/)

## International products with an Israel presence

All three are excellent, widely loved products in their home markets. In Israel their shared limitation is the same: **English-only, left-to-right UI, no Israeli tax-invoice (חשבונית מס / עוסק מורשה) compliance, foreign-currency billing, and no local Hebrew support.** They appear in Israeli listings (e.g. Capterra Israel) and some Israeli practitioners use them, but none is localized for Israel.

### SimplePractice (US) — mental-health category leader
- **Pricing:** Starter **$49/mo** (≈₪180) · Essential $79 (≈₪290) · Plus $99 (≈₪370). AI note-taker +$35/mo; ePrescribe +$49/mo plus $89 setup; additional clinician from +$74/mo. HIPAA-compliant.
- **Strengths:** Extremely polished; telehealth, client portal, intake paperwork, notes, scheduling, reminders, billing, secure messaging in one system; huge ecosystem; category leader for US mental-health practices.
- **Weaknesses in Israel:** English/LTR only, no Hebrew RTL; no Israeli tax-invoice compliance; USD billing; oriented to US insurance workflows; no local support.
- **Sources:** https://www.simplepractice.com/pricing/ · https://www.capterra.co.il/software/130710/simplepractice

### Jane (Jane App, Canada) — beloved allied-health system
- **Pricing:** roughly **$54–$99/mo** (≈₪200–₪370) depending on plan.
- **Strengths:** Outstanding, widely praised UX; online booking, telehealth, charting, and billing across many disciplines (physio, chiro, massage, naturopathy, mental health); strong scheduling.
- **Weaknesses in Israel:** English/LTR only, no Hebrew; no Israeli VAT/tax-invoice compliance; North-American billing/insurance orientation; no local support.
- **Sources:** https://jane.app/ · https://www.mentalyc.com/blog/simplepractice-vs-jane

### Cliniko (Australia) — full features at every tier
- **Pricing:** Per-practitioner tiers: **$45/mo** (1 practitioner, ≈₪165) · $95 (2–5) · $145 (6–8) · $195 (9–12) · $295 (13–25) · $395 (26–200). Every subscription includes the full feature set, unlimited users and locations. SMS ~$0.10 each. Annual billing gives one month free. 30-day free trial.
- **Strengths:** No feature gating — all plans get everything; unlimited locations; privacy-focused (Australian); telehealth, online booking, charting; scales cleanly from solo to large group.
- **Weaknesses in Israel:** English/LTR only, no Hebrew; no Israeli tax-invoice compliance; AUD/USD billing; no local support.
- **Sources:** https://www.cliniko.com/pricing/ · https://www.cliniko.com/

## Takeaways for the listicle

1. **Honest price ladder (Israeli, entry, ex-VAT):** CliniCloud ₪50 < Tipulog ₪75 < My-Cliniq ₪79 < (Medform ₪390, if verified). DOCTOR Clinix, Plexidoc, ClinicaOnline, and Jade are quote-only / undisclosed — say so plainly rather than guessing.
2. **Unlimited patients on the cheapest tier** is a real differentiator for CliniCloud and My-Cliniq; Tipulog's ₪75 tier caps at 20 patients.
3. **Best-fit routing is credible and fair:** solo/mobile → Jade; cheapest unlimited → CliniCloud; simple flat pricing → My-Cliniq; marketing+growth → Medform; brand + funder billing → Tipulog; enterprise/hospital depth → DOCTOR Clinix; physician/surgical → Plexidoc; vet or inventory/labs → ClinicaOnline; polished international workflows (non-IL-tax-sensitive) → SimplePractice/Jane/Cliniko.
4. **The wedge is structural, not a knock on quality:** every option here is cloud + subscription; the honest, verifiable ClinicFlow differentiators are offline/on-device, pay-once, data-stays-local, and תיקון 13 posture — plus, versus the international three, Hebrew/RTL and Israeli tax-invoice compliance. Keep competitor write-ups generous; let the model of ownership and data location carry the contrast.
