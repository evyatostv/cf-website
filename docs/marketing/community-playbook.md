## 0. The one rule above all: be a member, not an ad

The entire wedge of this product is *trust with the single most sensitive data category there
is.* You cannot buy that trust and you can destroy it in one spammy comment. So the rule that
overrides everything else in this doc:

**Give value first, disclose your affiliation always, and mention ClinicFlow only when it is a
genuinely relevant answer to what was actually asked — never as the opening move.**

Non-negotiables (breaking any of these can get you banned from a group *and* damage the brand):

- **Always disclose.** Any time ClinicFlow could benefit from your comment, you must say you
  are affiliated. No exceptions, no "soft launch" a comment and reveal later. Disclosure line
  in §3. This is both Facebook-group etiquette and Israeli consumer-protection law
  (חוק הגנת הצרכן — undisclosed commercial endorsement is deceptive advertising exposure).
- **Never sock-puppet.** No fake accounts, no asking friends/family/employees to "recommend"
  ClinicFlow, no pretending to be a happy customer, no seeding a question so you can answer it.
  All of this is fabricated social proof (`product-facts.md` §5.14) and it is forbidden.
- **Never DM-spam.** Do not slide into private messages to pitch unless a person explicitly
  asks you to DM them.
- **Respect group rules.** Many clinician groups ban promotion outright or restrict it to a
  weekly thread. Read the pinned rules before you post. If self-promo is banned, you may still
  answer questions helpfully *without* naming the product, or not engage at all. Never break a
  group's rules to get a mention in.
- **Stay inside approved claims.** Everything in `product-facts.md` §5 is off-limits here too:
  no SMS/WhatsApp/reminders-to-patients, no AI, no cloud, no mobile app, no API, no roles/
  multi-user, no kupot-cholim integration, no patient portal, no telehealth, no "50% fewer
  cancellations," no compliance guarantees, no ₪759, no version numbers, no customer counts.
- **Never argue or dunk on competitors by name.** Other tools are legitimate choices. Compete
  on the honest difference (offline, pay-once, data-stays-local), never by trashing a rival.

If a comment can't be genuinely useful *without* the product mention, don't post it.

## 1. Why Facebook groups (the strategic frame)

Israeli independent clinicians live in profession-specific Facebook groups. They ask each
other — not Google — "which software do you use?", "how do you back up patient files?",
"how do I open a private clinic?". These are exactly the questions where ClinicFlow's honest
differentiators (offline, one-time payment, data-stays-on-device, תיקון 13 framing) are the
*correct* answer for some people and irrelevant for others. That's the whole game: be the
helpful member who gives a straight answer, disclose that you're behind one of the options,
and let people decide.

What "winning" looks like here is **not** clicks this week. It's:
1. Becoming a recognizable, trusted, genuinely-helpful name in a few groups.
2. Being *remembered* when someone later searches "תוכנה לניהול קליניקה בלי מנוי."
3. Occasionally being the honest answer to a "which software" thread — with disclosure.

Volume is the enemy. Two thoughtful, disclosed, genuinely-useful comments a week beat twenty.

## 2. How to find the groups

### 2a. Search technique (do this once, build a list)

1. In Facebook search, type the Hebrew term, then filter **Groups** (קבוצות).
2. Sort/scan by **members** and, more importantly, **recent activity** (a 15k-member ghost
   town is worse than a 900-member active one). Open a group, check the last few posts' dates.
3. Read the group's **pinned rules** before joining-to-post. Note: promo-allowed? weekly promo
   thread? questions-only? Record it in the tracking sheet (§6).
4. Prefer **professional / peer** groups ("פסיכולוגים בישראל", "פיזיותרפיסטים") and
   **business-of-practice** groups ("קליניקה פרטית", "מטפלים עצמאיים") over pure
   client-facing "recommendations" groups.
5. Note whether the group is **public** (comments are indexable/screenshot-able — extra care)
   or **private** (norms are looser but still disclose).

### 2c. Where else the same people gather (adjacent)

- Professional-association member groups (per profession).
- "עוסק פטור / עוסק מורשה" and small-business groups — where "starting a private clinic"

- General "המלצות" groups **only** for direct "which software" asks, and only with disclosure.

### 2d. Which honest wedge resonates per profession (tailor the emphasis)

Same product, same approved claims — but different audiences care about different parts of the
offline / pay-once / data-stays-local story. Use this to decide *which* true differentiator to
lead with, and always name the honest anti-fit. Do **not** invent capabilities to match an
audience — if the fit isn't there, say so.

| Audience | Lead differentiator (all approved, `product-facts.md` §6) | Honest anti-fit to name |
|---|---|---|
| **פסיכולוגים / פסיכותרפיסטים / מטפלים ברגש** | Confidentiality of session content: data on the device only, no server, no cloud, company can't see it. | No access from a second device / no mobile — a phone-first therapist may prefer cloud. |
| **פיזיותרפיסטים / מרפאים בעיסוק / קלינאי תקשורת** | Works fully offline anywhere (home visits, basements with no signal); one-time price suits a solo practitioner. | No sync across devices; backup is on you. |
| **דיאטנים / תזונאים** | Structured documentation over time + receipts/invoices (advanced packages); pay-once, no monthly rent. | Receipts/invoices are in the advanced packages, not Basic. |
| **רופאים פרטיים / רופאי משפחה / אורתופדים** | Local encryption, SOAP visit documentation, professional opinions with signature + PDF. (Naming "AES-256" needs owner sign-off — `product-facts.md` §4 — so keep it to "encryption" in a comment.) | No kupot-cholim / national-records integration, no e-prescribing. |
| **וטרינרים** | Private-clinic basics pay-once: calendar, patient card, archive, and invoices in advanced packages; data stays local. | No online booking / patient portal. |

Whatever the audience, keep patient data and license activation distinct: patient data never

## 3. Rules of engagement

### 3a. The disclosure line (mandatory whenever CF could benefit)

Keep it short, human, up-front — not buried at the end:

> גילוי נאות: אני מאחורי ClinicFlow, אחת מהאפשרויות בתחום, אז יש לי אינטרס — אשתדל לתת תמונה
> הוגנת וגם להגיד איפה זה *לא* מתאים.

Variations by strength of pitch:
- **Answering a general "how do I…" question and CF is barely relevant:** a one-liner at the
  end — "(גילוי נאות: אני מפתח/ת של אחת התוכנות בתחום, אז קחו את זה בחשבון.)"
- **Directly recommending CF in a "which software" thread:** disclosure goes *first*, before
  you name it (Template 1).

### 3b. The helpful-first structure of every comment

1. **Answer the actual question** with real, vendor-neutral value the reader can use even if
   they never touch ClinicFlow. (This is 70–90% of the comment.)
2. **Disclose** affiliation.
3. **Soft, honest mention** — only if genuinely relevant — framed as "one option," with the
   one true differentiator that fits *their* question, and an honest note on who it's *not* for.
4. **No link unless asked**, or a bare "אשמח לשלוח פרטים במסר" if the group allows it. Public
   comments: prefer routing to a search of the brand name or info@clinic-flow.co.il over
   dropping URLs (some groups auto-remove link posts).

### 3c. Cadence & hygiene

- Space genuinely-helpful, non-promotional comments between promotional ones. Aim for a
  ratio where most of your footprint in a group is pure help with no product mention at all.
- Never post the same template verbatim twice in the same group, or across groups on the same
  day — rewrite in your own words each time. Copy-paste patterns read as spam and get filtered.
- One account, real name/identity, consistent across groups.
- If a thread turns into a pile-on or a competitor dispute, step out. Do not defend the brand
  by attacking anyone.
- **Correct yourself in public** if you ever state something slightly off — accuracy is the
  brand. Better to under-claim than to over-claim and get corrected by a member.

### 3d. Hard don'ts (community-specific, on top of §0)

- Don't answer your own planted question. Don't recruit others to recommend you.
- Don't offer discounts/gifts/anything for a mention, a recommendation, or a review (ties to
  `docs/marketing/review-generation.md` §0 — incentivized endorsements are forbidden).
- Don't screenshot or quote patients, cases, or anyone's clinical details.
- Don't give medical, legal, or tax/accounting advice as fact — frame as "worth checking with
  your accountant / professional body," especially in Templates 2 and 7.

## 5. Handling replies & follow-ups

- **Someone asks the price in a thread:** "החבילה הבסיסית מתחילה בתשלום חד־פעמי, המקצועית
  ₪999, והכול תשלום אחד בלי מנוי — אשמח לשלוח פירוט מלא במסר / במייל info@clinic-flow.co.il."
  Use ₪999 as the safe anchor; avoid the contested entry number in public. Never ₪759.
- **"יש אפליקציה לנייד?" / "עובד על מאק?":** don't wing it. "כדי לא להטעות אתכם אני מעדיף/ה
  לבדוק ולחזור אליכם במדויק — שלחו לי מסר ואוודא." Route to info@. (Mobile does not exist in
  the standard product; macOS is unresolved.)
- **"אפשר לשלוח למטופלים תזכורות SMS?":** answer honestly no — "התזכורות הן על המסך למטפל/ת,
  התוכנה לא שולחת הודעות למטופלים." Never imply messaging patients.
- **Anything about AI, cloud sync, kupot-cholim integration, team/staff accounts, patient
  login:** the honest answer is "זה לא חלק מהתוכנה" — say so plainly. Under-promising here
  is on-brand.
- **"אפשר להתקין על כמה מחשבים בקליניקה?" / seat questions:** don't over-specify licensing
  terms in public (ground truth: licensed per computer, but the pricing page doesn't restate
  seat rules). "הרישוי הוא לפי מחשב; לכמה עמדות בקליניקה — שלחו לי מסר/מייל ל-info@clinic-flow.co.il
  ואתן לכם את הפרטים המדויקים." Route to email rather than guess.
- **"מה קורה למידע אם החברה תיסגר / אתם נעלמים?":** the honest, reassuring truth — "המידע
  ממילא יושב על המחשב שלכם, לא אצלנו, ויש גיבוי מוצפן שבשליטתכם, אז הקבצים תמיד נשארים איתכם."
  Don't promise anything about the app continuing to activate; for licensing specifics route to
  info@clinic-flow.co.il.
- **"יש תקופת ניסיון / דמו?":** don't invent a free trial — it isn't a confirmed feature. Say
  what *is* true: "יש 30 יום החזר כספי מלא, אז אפשר להתנסות בלי סיכון — ואם לא מתאים, מקבלים
  את הכסף בחזרה." (approved claim, `product-facts.md` §6). For a walkthrough, route to email.
- **A negative or skeptical reply:** thank them, don't argue, offer to take specifics to
  info@clinic-flow.co.il. A calm, non-defensive public reply is worth more than winning.

## 7. Quick do / don't

- **DO** join the right groups (§2), read the rules, and be genuinely useful most of the time.
- **DO** disclose affiliation on every mention, up-front, in your own words.
- **DO** lead with the honest wedge — offline, one-time payment, data-stays-on-device,
  תיקון-13 framing — and name who it's *not* for.
- **DO** route price detail, mac/mobile questions, and complaints to info@clinic-flow.co.il.
- **DON'T** sock-puppet, plant questions, DM-spam, incentivize mentions, or fake being a customer.
- **DON'T** break a group's promo rules to squeeze in a mention.
- **DON'T** state forbidden claims: SMS/WhatsApp/reminders-to-patients, AI, cloud, mobile app,
  API, roles/multi-user, kupot-cholim, patient portal, telehealth, "50% fewer cancellations,"
  compliance guarantees, ₪759, or version numbers.
- **DON'T** trash competitors or argue publicly — step out instead.
